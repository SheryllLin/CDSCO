Regulatory Workflow Automation Demo Use Cases

Usage note: These examples are for reviewer support and system demonstration only. The tool should produce draft analysis, suggested prioritisation, potential duplicates, and draft reports. It must not be treated as a final medical, legal, or regulatory decision-maker.

Use Case 1: SAE-Style Adverse Event Review

Primary Narrative / Submission Text

Patient Ms. Kavita Sharma, female, 45 years old, reported severe skin rash and breathing difficulty after taking DrugX for 3 days. She was admitted to City Care Hospital for observation. Case ID: SAE-101. Reporter: Dr. R. Mehta. The event improved after stopping the drug. Follow-up causality assessment is pending.

Previous Version Text

Patient reported skin rash after taking DrugX. Case ID: SAE-101. Follow-up review is pending.

Duplicate Candidate Documents

DOC-1::Patient Ms. Kavita Sharma, female, 45 years old, reported severe skin rash and breathing difficulty after taking DrugX for 3 days. She was admitted to City Care Hospital for observation. DOC-2::Patient Kavita Sharma reported severe rash and breathing difficulty after DrugX and was admitted to City Care Hospital. DOC-3::Patient reported mild nausea after DrugY and was treated at home.

Use Case 2: Hospitalization Support Review

Primary Narrative / Submission Text

Patient Mr. Arun Nair, male, 63 years old, developed chest discomfort, dizziness, and low blood pressure after receiving ProductA. He was hospitalized for monitoring in Sunrise Medical College. Case ID: SAE-102. Reporter: Dr. Neha Jain. The patient was stabilized within 24 hours and discharged with advice for follow-up.

Previous Version Text

Patient developed dizziness after receiving ProductA. Case ID: SAE-102. Monitoring was advised.

Duplicate Candidate Documents

DOC-1::Patient developed chest discomfort and dizziness after ProductA and was hospitalized for monitoring. DOC-2::Patient developed chest discomfort and dizziness after ProductA and was admitted for monitoring. DOC-3::Patient had headache after ProductB and did not require admission.

Structured Form Data

Use Case 3: Clinical Application Completeness Check

Frontend settings: Document Type = application, Checklist Type = clinical_application

Primary Narrative / Submission Text

Application ID: CTA-201. Applicant: ABC Pharma Pvt Ltd. Study Title: Phase III clinical study for DrugM in uncontrolled hypertension. Indication: Hypertension. Site Count: 12. Ethics Committee: Approved. The submission includes protocol synopsis, investigator brochure, and insurance summary. However, one checklist annexure is still pending.

Previous Version Text

Application ID: CTA-201. Applicant: ABC Pharma Pvt Ltd. Study Title: clinical study for DrugM in hypertension. Site Count: 10. Ethics Committee: Under review.

Duplicate Candidate Documents

DOC-1::Application ID CTA-201 for DrugM in hypertension with 12 sites and ethics approval. DOC-2::CTA-201 submission for DrugM in uncontrolled hypertension with 12 sites and ethics approval.

Use Case 4: Meeting Summary Synthesis

Frontend settings: Document Type = meeting

Primary Narrative / Submission Text

The committee reviewed the safety update for DrugQ and discussed two unresolved questions on patient monitoring. Members agreed that the sponsor should submit a revised risk management note within 7 days. The protocol deviation issue was noted but not considered critical. Next meeting will review the revised submission and assign follow-up actions.

Previous Version Text

The committee reviewed the safety update for DrugQ. Follow-up information from the sponsor was requested.

Duplicate Candidate Documents

DOC-1::Committee reviewed DrugQ safety update and asked for revised risk management note. DOC-2::Meeting discussed DrugQ safety and requested revised risk management note within 7 days.

Structured Form Data

{ "case_id": "MTG-301", "reporter_name": "Internal Committee Note", "suspect_product": "DrugQ" }

Use Case 5: Inspection Report Draft

Frontend settings: Document Type = inspection

Primary Narrative / Submission Text

During inspection, the team observed missing signatures on two batch records and incomplete equipment cleaning logs. One storage temperature record was not updated for the previous day. Staff stated that corrective action would be submitted within 5 working days. No evidence of product mix-up was found during the visit.

Previous Version Text

Inspection noted missing signatures on batch records. Corrective action was requested.
